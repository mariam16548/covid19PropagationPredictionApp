PROJECT NARRATIVE

We propose a Shiny web app that will help members of the public answer the following question: "Will your actions today likely propagate COVID-19 to your community?". Our app will answer this question using the population and number of cases in a given county. The target audience for our application will be college students who are likely living on campus and socializing with others. When faced with only a vague sense of the risk, people can make poor COVID-related choices. In our app, we quantify the risk by combining local data with their social plans, which helps people make informed decisions. This is done through software combining epidemiological and population data, modeling behavior (alcohol consumption, mask wear, number of participants) to calculate risk in an easy-to-use interface rendered in vivid display.

COVID-19 is a large concern nationally, especially for college students. Colleges across the country have opened their campuses to students this fall, resulting in more opportunities for students to socially gather. At the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, partying and other social events were big factors in the increased COVID-19 cases. We wanted to find a way to investigate how alcohol consumption as well as other factors can decrease awareness and negatively affect social distancing procedures. We came up with a Shiny app, which allows users to create interactive web applications, to look into this further. This project applies engineering design to produce solutions that meet specified needs with consideration of public health and safety, as well as social and environmental factors to encourage social distancing measures. When the risk is calculated to be “extremely high” and the message of “stay home!” appears, the user will be more inclined to listen because of the personalized experience the web app provides.

Plan of Operation

    Project design

We have a prototype that predicts the likelihood of contagious spread through two factors: county population (people per square mile) and the number of cases in a given county. We use the latest COVID-19 statistics that are obtained in real-time through  Knowi's public data access API The app combines empirical data (population, case counts, transmission rates) with individual social circumstances (group size, age, alcohol use, mask wear, indoors/outdoors) to predict the risk of COVID-19 spread from a planned social activity. We use a variety of models (see "Modeling and Implementation Details" section) and we produce a range of risk estimates. From this, users of the app are able to predict the consequences of their actions.

 

    Modeling and Implementation Details

The COVID-19 data is currently only available at the county level. In order to make sure that the users are able to use their zip code, we used the U.S. Census data to link the zip code to the appropriate county. From there, it made it easier to then find the corresponding statistics. In order to produce an estimate for the risk I used the following sources:

    • MASK WEAR: According to this paper, wearing a mask lowers an individual's risk of contracting the disease by 65%. 

    • ALCOHOL CONSUMPTION: In this paper, it was found that a .08 BAC resulted in an average of 66% impairment in the form of lowered awareness, which would therefore make it more difficult to maintain social distancing measures.

    • AGE:
    • This survey found that 45% of people under 30 are socializing with people they do not live with and not social distancing
    • This study found that around 40% of COVID cases are from people under 30. 
    • Therefore, around 16% of young people (likely asymptomatic) are engaging in risky behavior (.40*.45)
    • There is an 82% risk reduction when social distancing
    • 1-(.82*(1-.16)) = .31 →  31% risk increase for young people under the age of 30 

    • GROUP SIZE: We followed the same method as this COVID-19 event risk assessment calculator.
